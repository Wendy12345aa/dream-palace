# Core Memory

## Identity

- Project: Dream Palace.
- Form: character-driven Living Kingdom and court-life narrative strategy game.
- Player fantasy: become a leader people willingly choose to follow.
- Emotional promise: "I was not managing a kingdom. I was leading people who believed in me."
- The player has no fixed face or gender. The throne belongs to the player.

## Pillars

1. People before systems.
2. Morning Court is the core experience and living home screen.
3. Companions own gameplay and major interfaces.
4. Decisions have visible world and relationship consequences.
5. The kingdom feels alive before, during, and after player input.
6. Leadership, trust, and responsibility matter more than power.

## Core Loop

`Morning Court -> report -> disagreement -> player investigation/decision -> companion action -> visible change -> reflection -> tomorrow hook`

## Non-Negotiables

- Context before interface.
- Characters before feature count.
- Visible feedback before hidden calculations.
- Warm, hopeful Eastern fantasy before grim spectacle.
- New features deepen relationships or participation before expanding scope.
- The player participates in governing; they do not merely watch companions govern.

## Never Default To

- Generic dashboard, card grid, side-panel administration, or mobile page-game UI.
- Generic action RPG, battle-first loop, equipment grind, or MMO feature checklist.
- A traditional world map as the primary interface.
- Long lore dumps, filler dialogue, or all seven companions introduced at once.
- Fixed player appearance.
- Real AI, 3D, Live2D, backend, or networking without a validated experience need.

## Feature Test

Approve a feature only if most answers are yes:

1. Does it strengthen a companion relationship or point of view?
2. Does the player make or discover something meaningful?
3. Does the kingdom respond visibly?
4. Does it belong naturally inside the world?
5. Will players remember the moment rather than the menu?
