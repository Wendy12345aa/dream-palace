# Demo Memory

## Contract

- Title: Dream Palace: The First Morning Court.
- Goal: make players want to return for another Morning Court.
- Prove: characters drive systems, decisions change the kingdom visibly, and the court feels alive.
- Original Bible target: 15-20 minutes.
- Near-term playtest target: a dense 8-10 minute single route without filler.

## Current Flow

1. Language choice.
2. Eight-shot, roughly 43-second opening; skippable.
3. Player enters an already-active Morning Court.
4. MP presents Northbridge's grain and damaged-bridge petition.
5. Tab describes risk; Kel proposes a temporary repair.
6. Player opens MP's reserve ledger.
7. Player fits Kel's support beam to the broken span.
8. Inspection reveals rusted pins: the damage predates last night.
9. Player places a grain cart or audit flag on Northbridge.
10. Kingdom Table shows an immediate visible consequence.
11. Companion responses and evening reflection.
12. CX reveals the repair history may be suspicious; tomorrow hook.

## Active Cast

- MP: treasury, responsibility, human cost.
- Tab: standards, risk, accountability.
- Kel: engineering, visible action, hope.
- CX: ending investigation hook only.

## Decision Branches

- Release grain: protect villagers now, spend reserve, begin temporary repair.
- Audit first: protect reserve and investigate, but villagers wait.
- Neither route is perfect or treated as failure.

## Interaction Rules

- Keep interaction inside the Kingdom Table; avoid separate minigame screens.
- Prefer physical verbs: open, drag, place, inspect, unfold, stamp.
- Failure or misplacement returns an object; it does not produce Game Over.
- The interaction must reveal information or express a decision, not exist as busywork.
- Preserve keyboard/tap accessibility for drag actions.

## Success Questions

- Who did the player remember?
- What did MP, Tab, and Kel each want?
- What new fact did the bridge inspection reveal?
- What changed after the decision?
- Was any input unclear?
- Did the CX hook create interest in the next morning?

## Avoid

- Second event before testing the first.
- More dialogue merely to increase runtime.
- Combat opening, lore dump, full economy, or all seven companions.
- Detached puzzle/minigame transitions.
