# World Memory

## World Identity

- Eastern fantasy kingdom with grounded civic problems and light fantasy potential.
- Hopeful and lived-in rather than grim or purely historical.
- The kingdom continues changing beyond direct player input.
- History is produced by decisions, resource flow, relationships, and visible consequences.

## World Interface

- The Morning Court is where the world speaks through people.
- The Kingdom Table is a physical model/workbench used to inspect and change regions.
- A map is supporting evidence or visible consequence, not the default menu.
- Regions should be introduced through a person, request, route, or consequence.

## Canonical Demo Location

Northbridge / Northbridge Village / Chinese: `北桥村`.

Current facts:

- A northern village depends on an old supply bridge.
- The bridge is damaged before harvest and grain delivery is at risk.
- The village requests emergency grain.
- Kel's inspection reveals rusted iron pins; the failure began before the reported incident.
- Prior repair history is suspicious and motivates CX's next-morning investigation.

Do not rename Northbridge casually or turn it into a city, faction, or unrelated location.

## Resources

Core resource language may include gold, grain, wood, and iron, but resources exist to create human trade-offs and visible change. Avoid economy design that becomes detached spreadsheet optimization.

## Threat Tone

- Prefer grounded shortages, corruption, infrastructure failure, rumor, weather, faction pressure, espionage, and border conflict.
- Fantasy threats must fit the Eastern setting and affect governance.
- Do not default to goblins, generic demon armies, or a world-ending first crisis.

## World Test

- Who is affected?
- Which companion understands the issue?
- What can the player inspect or decide?
- What visibly changes afterward?
- How will the court remember it tomorrow?
