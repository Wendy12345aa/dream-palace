# Dream Palace Art Rules

Use these rules for every Dream Palace visual.

## Core Identity

Dream Palace is a character-driven Living Kingdom RPG. Art should make the kingdom feel alive before a single word is spoken.

The target feeling is warm, cinematic, elegant, lived-in, hopeful, and companion-centered. Avoid grimdark, generic fantasy, page-game, mobile dashboard, or admin-backend composition.

## Dream Palace Signature

Every screenshot or asset should satisfy at least three:

- Companion visible, or a strong human presence when no companion is relevant.
- Warm light.
- Visible kingdom history.
- Living background.
- Emotional focus.
- Camera storytelling.
- Minimal UI.
- Environmental motion.

## Visual Principles

- Characters before environment.
- Warm before dark.
- Elegant simplicity.
- Living spaces that remember history.
- Minimal interface presence.
- Clear silhouettes.
- Restrained ornament.
- Natural materials, sky, air, sunlight, paper, ink, wood, fabric, lanterns.

## Color Identity

Use companion colors as accents, not one-note full-screen palettes:

- MP: royal gold, trust, responsibility, warmth, stewardship.
- Kel: emerald green, growth, engineering, creation.
- Tab: azure blue, discipline, order, precision, approval.
- CX: dark purple, mystery, investigation, hidden truth, risk.
- Mika: soft pink and light blue, hope, culture, beauty, imagination.
- Vincey: orange, energy, celebration, public emotion.
- Shian: silver, wisdom, strategy, structure.

## Camera Language

- Wide shot: kingdom scale, court composition, emotional distance.
- Medium shot: conversation, relationship, shared decision-making.
- Close-up: decision, trust, doubt, pressure.
- Reaction shot: companion emotion, consequence, memory.
- Environment shot: world change, visible consequence, kingdom history.

## Avoid

- Generic fantasy castles.
- Excessively dark palettes.
- UI covering the artwork.
- Over-designed armor.
- Visual clutter.
- Excessive particles.
- Static environments.
- Lifeless crowds.
- Mobile dashboard composition.
- Traditional page-game layout.
- One-note color palettes.
- Character lineups unless explicitly requested as a key visual.

## First Impression Rule

The viewer should recognize Dream Palace within three seconds because of atmosphere, not logo.
