# Dream Palace Project Vision

Dream Palace exists to make the player believe they are governing a living kingdom together with seven companions.

The game is not about optimizing menus.

It is about entering the Morning Court, hearing people argue for the future, making a decision, and watching the world remember it.

---

## Why This Project Exists

Many kingdom games make the player feel like an administrator.

Dream Palace should make the player feel like a ruler surrounded by people who matter.

The fantasy is not absolute control.

The fantasy is shared governance: the throne belongs to the player, but the kingdom belongs to everyone.

---

## Why the Morning Court Matters

The Morning Court is the emotional center of Dream Palace.

It is where the player meets the kingdom through people instead of menus.

Every important system should eventually be able to appear in the Morning Court as a human problem:

- a treasury concern brought by MP,
- a research proposal brought by Kel,
- an investigation warning brought by CX,
- a quality failure brought by Tab,
- a public event brought by Vincey,
- a cultural question brought by Mika,
- a planning conflict brought by Shian.

If a feature cannot become a meaningful court conversation, it may not belong in Dream Palace.

---

## Why UI Belongs to Characters

Dream Palace should not treat UI as a neutral layer.

A major interface is a relationship with a companion.

The treasury is not only a budget screen; it is MP's way of seeing the kingdom.

Technology is not only a tech tree; it is Kel's hope, impatience, and invention logic.

Planning is not only a calendar; it is Shian's discipline and anxiety about the future.

This rule protects the project from becoming a generic management game.

---

## Why the Player Has No Fixed Appearance

The player is the presence on the throne.

Dream Palace should avoid over-defining the ruler when doing so would weaken projection, roleplay, or emotional ownership.

The player should feel recognized by the companions through decisions, trust, and consequences rather than through a fixed face.

---

## Why Dream Palace Avoids Traditional Quest Thinking

Dream Palace should not reduce the kingdom to a checklist of quests.

A problem should feel like a pressure on the kingdom, a disagreement between people, or a consequence of earlier choices.

The player is not clearing content.

The player is shaping a living world.

---

## The Core Development Question

Do not ask first:

> Is this feature cool?

Ask:

> Will this make the player believe they are governing a country together with MP, Kel, Tab, CX, Mika, Vincey, and Shian?

If the answer is yes, it belongs to Dream Palace.

If the answer is only that it is cool, it probably belongs to another game.
