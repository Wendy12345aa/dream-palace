# Chapter 3 — Gameplay Bible

> *Every day in Dream Palace should feel like another meaningful day in a living kingdom.*

---

# Purpose

The World Bible defines how the kingdom lives.

The Gameplay Bible defines how the player lives within that kingdom.

This document explains the player's daily experience, long-term progression, and the gameplay systems that create meaningful leadership.

Every gameplay system should reinforce the Dream Palace vision.

---

# Core Gameplay Philosophy

Dream Palace is not a game about doing more tasks.

It is a game about making better decisions.

Players should feel that every action influences people, the kingdom, and the future.

Gameplay exists to create stories rather than routines.

---

# The Daily Loop

Every day follows the same emotional rhythm.

Morning Court

↓

Receive reports

↓

Discuss with companions

↓

Make decisions

↓

Explore or act

↓

Observe consequences

↓

Prepare for tomorrow

The loop should feel familiar without becoming repetitive.

---

# The Morning Court

The Morning Court is the heart of Dream Palace.

Every in-game day begins here.

Companions report on:

- treasury
- construction
- technology
- investigations
- public morale
- events
- long-term strategy

Players should never feel they are reading a dashboard.

They should feel they are attending court.

---

# Leadership Gameplay

Players rarely solve problems directly.

Instead, they:

- approve
- reject
- prioritize
- negotiate
- delegate
- encourage
- intervene

Leadership should create gameplay.

Not simply dialogue.

---

# Exploration

Players may leave the court.

Exploration exists to understand the kingdom.

Possible destinations include:

- cities
- villages
- forests
- construction sites
- faction headquarters
- festivals
- disaster locations

Exploration should reveal stories, not only rewards.

---

# Companion Gameplay

Every companion should create a unique gameplay style.

MP

Managing treasury and allocation.

Kel

Research, engineering, and infrastructure.

Tab

Audits, quality, and administration.

CX

Investigation, intelligence, and hidden events.

Mika

Culture, morale, and public identity.

Vincey

Festivals, diplomacy, and ceremonies.

Shian

Long-term planning, logistics, and expansion.

Players should naturally rotate between companions rather than repeatedly opening generic menus.

---

# Decision System

Most gameplay revolves around decisions.

Every important decision should include:

- incomplete information
- trade-offs
- different companion opinions
- visible consequences

There should rarely be a perfect answer.

---

# Adventure

The ruler does not personally solve every problem.

Instead, adventures represent exceptional moments.

Examples:

- investigating corruption
- negotiating peace
- inspecting disasters
- diplomatic visits
- protecting important people

Adventure should complement leadership rather than replace it.

---

# Kingdom Growth

Growth should happen naturally.

Example:

Better roads

↓

Faster logistics

↓

Lower food prices

↓

Higher citizen trust

↓

More stable kingdom

Growth should always create visible change.

---

# MMORPG Features

Multiplayer exists because kingdoms share a world.

Players may:

- trade
- cooperate
- compete
- form alliances
- create guilds
- influence regional politics

The MMO layer should strengthen the living world rather than distract from it.

---

# Progression

Players grow through leadership.

Examples:

- companion trust
- kingdom prosperity
- regional influence
- faction reputation
- technology
- public support

Progress should feel earned through decisions.

Not grinding.

---

# Failure

Failure should move the story forward.

Bad decisions create new opportunities.

Companions react.

Citizens remember.

The kingdom changes.

Avoid hard fail states whenever possible.

---

# Gameplay Principles

Every gameplay system should:

- create conversation
- create consequence
- create emotional memory
- strengthen companions
- strengthen the kingdom

Avoid gameplay that exists only to increase playtime.

---

# Gameplay Test

When evaluating a feature, ask:

- Does this create leadership?
- Does this create relationships?
- Does this create memorable consequences?
- Does this create a living kingdom?

If not, it probably does not belong in Dream Palace.

---

# Final Reminder

Players should return because they miss the kingdom.

Not because they fear missing rewards.

---

> **Great kingdoms are not built in one day.**
>
> **They are built one meaningful day at a time.**
