# Chapter 6 — Art Bible

> *Art should make the kingdom feel alive before a single word is spoken.*

---

# Purpose

The UI / UX Bible defines how players interact with the kingdom.

The Art Bible defines how the kingdom is seen and felt.

Art is not decoration.

Art is storytelling.

Every illustration, environment, animation, color, and visual effect should strengthen the Dream Palace experience.

---

# Art Philosophy

Dream Palace should never look like a traditional management game.

It should never feel like a mobile game dashboard.

Instead, players should feel that they have stepped inside a living animated kingdom.

Every visual should communicate warmth, history, and companionship.

The visual goal is not only to look beautiful.

The visual goal is to make players believe this kingdom has people worth returning to.

---

# Visual Identity

Dream Palace borrows design philosophy, not visual style.

Inspirations may guide ambition, pacing, clarity, emotional presentation, and polish, but Dream Palace must remain an original IP with its own visual identity.

## Emotional Storytelling

Inspired by:

- Persona 5
- Clair Obscur: Expedition 33

Focus:

- Cinematic framing
- Emotional lighting
- Expressive reactions
- Strong character presence

---

## Character Presence

Inspired by:

- Zenless Zone Zero
- Marvel Rivals

Characters dominate the experience.

Menus exist behind them.

Not the opposite.

The player should remember who they spoke with before remembering which menu they opened.

---

## Court Atmosphere

Inspired by:

- Metaphor: ReFantazio

Focus:

- Ceremonial halls
- Morning Court
- Political discussion
- Warm architecture
- Living backgrounds
- Companions gathered around the throne

---

## Presentation Quality

Inspired by:

- Honkai: Star Rail

Focus:

- Modern transitions
- High-quality illustration
- Elegant motion
- Minimal visual clutter
- Clean composition

---

# Core Visual Principles

## Characters Before Environment

Players should notice companions first.

Backgrounds support people.

They should not replace them.

A beautiful room is only successful if it makes the companions feel more present.

---

## Warm Before Dark

Dream Palace is not grim fantasy.

Even difficult moments should contain hope.

Use:

- Warm light
- Natural materials
- Soft colors
- Comfortable interiors
- Air, sky, and sunlight

Dark scenes should feel tense, not depressing.

---

## Elegant Simplicity

Reduce unnecessary ornament.

Keep silhouettes clear.

Every object should have purpose.

Avoid visual noise.

The screen should feel designed, not filled.

---

## Living Spaces

Rooms should change over time.

Books accumulate.

Plants grow.

Construction appears.

Maps gain notes.

Lanterns increase.

The kingdom should visibly remember history.

---

## Minimal Interface Presence

Art should not be buried under UI.

A screenshot should still feel like Dream Palace even before labels, meters, and buttons appear.

The world and companions carry the first impression.

---

---

---

---

# Character Art Philosophy

Companions must be recognizable at a glance.

Each character should have:

- A clear silhouette
- A distinct color identity
- A recognizable prop or visual symbol
- Expression range
- Costume details tied to their kingdom responsibility

Character art should support gameplay identity.

MP should visually belong to treasury and stewardship.

Kel should visually belong to engineering and invention.

Tab should visually belong to discipline and review.

CX should visually belong to secrecy and intelligence.

Mika should visually belong to culture and beauty.

Vincey should visually belong to celebration and public emotion.

Shian should visually belong to planning and strategy.

---

# Illustration Philosophy

Illustrations should feel like key memories.

Examples:

- Morning Court assembled
- Lantern Festival
- First Harvest
- Kingdom survives winter
- Companion reunion
- A rebuilt city at sunrise
- A quiet treasury after a hard decision

Victory should feel personal.

Not merely successful.

---

# Animation Philosophy

Animation communicates life.

Use small motion to make the world breathe.

Examples:

- Clothing reacts to movement
- Lanterns sway
- Trees move
- Paper unfolds
- Ink dries
- Construction progresses
- Characters breathe naturally
- Dust catches sunlight
- Curtains move with wind

Small motion creates immersion.

Large motion should be reserved for important emotional moments.

---

# Effects Philosophy

Effects should support emotion.

Avoid excessive particles.

Use:

- Sunlight
- Dust
- Petals
- Wind
- Rain
- Candlelight
- Snow
- Ink
- Paper

Nature should become part of storytelling.

Effects should never distract from characters.

---

---

---

# Dream Palace Signature

Every screenshot should satisfy at least three of the following:

- Companion visible
- Warm light
- Visible kingdom history
- Living background
- Emotional focus
- Camera storytelling
- Minimal UI
- Environmental motion

If a screenshot does not satisfy enough of these traits, it may look beautiful but still fail to feel like Dream Palace.

---

# First Impression Rule

A player should recognize Dream Palace within three seconds.

Not because of the logo.

Because of the atmosphere.

The throne, the Morning Court, the warm light, the companions, the sense of a kingdom that remembers its people: these should create recognition before branding appears.

---

# Key Visual Goal

Dream Palace should eventually share one official key visual across GitHub, Steam, the website, and pitch materials.

The target first impression is:

1. Logo.
2. Official key visual.
3. **Build a Living Kingdom.**

The key visual should communicate:

- The empty throne
- The Morning Court
- The seven companions
- Warm light
- A kingdom worth returning to

The player should understand that the throne is theirs before seeing the player character.

---

# Art Test

Ask:

Does this image feel warm?

Does this image feel lived in?

Can players recognize the companion immediately?

Does the screenshot feel like Dream Palace without a logo?

Would players believe this kingdom has history?

Would players want to return here tomorrow?

If not, the art should be reconsidered.

---

# Things We Avoid

Avoid:

- Generic fantasy castles
- Excessively dark palettes
- UI covering the artwork
- Over-designed armor
- Visual clutter
- Excessive particle effects
- Static environments
- Lifeless NPC crowds
- Mobile dashboard composition
- Traditional page-game layout
- One-note color palettes

Art should always serve emotion.

Not spectacle.

---

# Final Reminder

Players should remember places because people lived there.

Players should remember companions because they changed those places.

Beautiful art attracts players.

Meaningful art makes them stay.

---
